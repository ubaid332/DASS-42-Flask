What is DASS-12?

Introduction

DASS-12 refers to a shortened version of the Depression, Anxiety, and Stress Scale (DASS). The DASS-12 is a concise version of the original DASS questionnaire, which consists of 42 items (14 items per subscale). The DASS-12 retains four items from each subscale, resulting in a total of 12 items (hence the name DASS-12).

Purpose

The DASS-12 items are carefully selected to capture the key aspects of each subscale and maintain the validity and reliability of the original DASS. Respondents rate the frequency and intensity of their experiences over the past week using a Likert scale. The scores on each subscale (depression, anxiety, and stress) are computed based on the responses to the respective items.

What is Quick Test?

Introduction

The purpose of the DASS Quick Test is to provide a very brief assessment of general distress levels, allowing for a quick screening or initial evaluation of emotional well-being. It is particularly useful in situations where time is limited, and a brief measure is needed.
